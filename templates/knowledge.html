📚 Smart Farming Knowledge Base

- Choose crops based on soil type and rainfall patterns.
- Use crop rotation to maintain soil fertility.
- Prefer high-yield and disease-resistant crop varieties.

- Test soil pH regularly and apply lime or gypsum as required.
- Use organic manure and compost for better fertility.
- Avoid overuse of chemical fertilizers to protect soil microbes.

- Adopt drip irrigation for water efficiency.
- Water crops early in the morning or late evening to reduce evaporation.
- Ensure proper drainage to avoid waterlogging.

- Rotate crops to break pest cycles.
- Use biological control methods like neem oil.
- Monitor fields regularly to detect early signs of infestation.

- Store grains in airtight containers to prevent pest damage.
- Sell crops in local markets with higher demand.
- Stay updated with government schemes and support prices.
